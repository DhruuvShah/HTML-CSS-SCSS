Our story

Started with one espresso machine and a folding table.

Kiln began in 2019 as a six-seat counter with a second-hand La Marzocco and a folding table for a bar. No investors, no business plan — just a conviction that Mumbai deserved coffee that tasted of where it came from, not just how dark it was roasted. Seven years on, that conviction hasn't changed; only the size of the roaster has.

How we got here

A short history of a slow obsession

  • 2019

    The folding-table counter

    Opened a six-seat counter on Lamington Road with a second-hand espresso machine and beans bought from three different importers, none of which we trusted completely.

  • 2021

    We started roasting our own

    After outgrowing three suppliers in two years, we bought a 1958 Probat drum roaster and began roasting in-house — twice a week, in batches small enough to taste every one.

  • 2023

    The roastery & cupping lab opened

    Moved roasting to a dedicated space two streets away, built a proper cupping table, and signed direct-trade agreements with three family farms in Ethiopia, Colombia and Sumatra.

  • 2026

    Where we are today

    One roastery, one counter, a waitlist for our subscription — and the same six seats we started with, because some things are worth keeping small.

What guides us

Three things we won't compromise on

People before margins

We pay our farms above fair-trade rates and our baristas above industry average. Good coffee starts with people who aren't worried about rent.

Provenance over branding

Every bag tells you the farm, the harvest date and the roast date — no blends with vague names hiding where the beans actually came from.

Patience over volume

We roast small batches, rest them properly, and stop selling a coffee the moment it's past its best — even if that means running out before the weekend.

Visual-storyteller's plan for this portfolio
Right now the site shows great work — but it doesn't yet narrate it. A few additions could turn this from "a gallery of images" into "a story you scroll through." Here's what I'd prioritize, in story order:

1. Chapter markers / scene transitions
Right now "2025. The Beginning" and "2026. Escalation" are just section headers — but they read like chapter titles in someone's creative journey. I'd lean into that: large full-bleed "interstitial" moments between major sections — a beat of negative space with a single line of kinetic typography (e.g. "Then came the work that shaped me—") that animates in/out on scroll. This turns section boundaries into narrative beats rather than just visual breaks.

2. Captions that surface on the image grids
The new b1–b9 grid images are purely decorative right now — no names, no context. A storyteller would want each one to mean something. On hover (desktop) / scroll-reveal (mobile), a small caption (project name · year · one-line description) could slide up over each image — same visual language as the testimonial cards, just compressed. Suddenly the grid becomes a body of work, not wallpaper.

3. A journey timeline / milestone spine
The site already has implicit milestones — Lotus (2017, Mumbai) → Lavender (2022, Paris) → Palm (2025, Saudi) → "2025: The Beginning" → "2026: Escalation." I'd add a thin animated vertical line (or horizontal on scroll) connecting these as literal waypoints — each one pulses/lights up as it scrolls into view. This is the connective tissue that makes disparate sections feel like one continuous story of growth.

4. One "spotlight" case study, told in motion
Rather than every project getting equal weight, pick one (Lotus, since it's the oldest/most foundational) and give it a richer scroll-driven mini-sequence: a few process/detail shots that crossfade or pin-and-scrub as you scroll past, paired with a short narrative ("It started as a sketch on a train…"). This is the centerpiece moment — the thing that makes someone stop scrolling and pay attention.

5. An opening title sequence
Before the hero text reveals, a brief (1–1.5s) branded "curtain" — the kind of thing that signals "this is a crafted experience" the instant the page loads. Logo mark or initials assembling/splitting, then dissolving into the existing hero reveal. Sets the tone before a single word is read.

6. Editorial pull-quotes
Pull one striking line from each testimonial ("Lotus gave our brand a soul it didn't know it was missing — Mumbai, 2017") and present it as oversized, italic editorial type between project blocks — breaking the rhythm of image-heavy sections with voice.
